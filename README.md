## What's Actually Running Under the Hood

This matters more than most people think when comparing budget hosts. DediRock uses **OpenNebula** cloud infrastructure for resource isolation, which handles VM management better than a lot of older setups in this price tier. All KVM VPS plans come with:

- True KVM virtualization (not OpenVZ)
- Full root access
- Dedicated IPv4 address
- 1 Gbps connection (on KVM plans)
- Your choice of major Linux distributions: Ubuntu, Debian, CentOS, AlmaLinux, Rocky Linux, and more

The LA data center sits near One Wilshire in Los Angeles — one of the most well-connected facilities on the West Coast. The Buffalo location handles East Coast coverage and, interestingly, tends to get slightly better marks for network stability in community discussions, even though LA has the edge for global latency.

## What People Are Actually Saying

Reviews on Trustpilot give DediRock around a 4-star average with 31 reviews. The picture that emerges is pretty honest and consistent with what you'd expect from a budget provider that's actively building.

**The good stuff that keeps coming up:**

One buyer who grabbed two VPS servers at an annual rate praised the double bandwidth and clean setup process — said the servers ran smoothly with responsive support when needed. The community has also noted that DediRock's founder Danny personally responds to virtually every review and support issue, which isn't something you see at corporate hosting providers where you're bounced between tier-one support scripts.

Community benchmarks from late 2025 showed LA VPS disk speeds around 45 MB/s on 4K mixed read/write and scaling to 6.87 GB/s on 1 MB block sizes. Network hit 920 Mbps on local LA tests.

**The honest caveats:**

There was a storage node incident in early 2026 — simultaneous hard drive and RAID controller failure — that caused data loss for customers on that node. DediRock migrated affected customers to new VPSs, though some felt the communication during the incident could have been faster. The control panel (WHMCS + Virtualizor) is functional but not sleek. One reviewer called it "from the stone ages," which is a bit harsh but not entirely wrong — it works, it's just not pretty. The LA location has seen occasional congestion during peak hours, while Buffalo tends to be more consistent for East Coast use.

The honest summary from the community is pretty clear: DediRock is the right pick for developers running test environments, anyone self-hosting apps like Nextcloud or a personal VPN, small website owners upgrading from shared hosting, and backup/archive storage. If you're running a production app where every minute of downtime is a financial event, you'd probably want a more established provider with documented SLAs.
